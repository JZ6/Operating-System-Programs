Describe the fourth program of your choice and explain what you found interesting about its memory reference behaviour:

    The Fourth program of my choice is what I call a midterm simulator. Basically, there are calm periods
where very little memory referencing happens, and then there are the stressed periods where information
is crammed and retrieved at a very rapid pace, followed finally by a calm period again. This program is
interesting because I can model my study habits based on the results of this program, where my options
are: study random materials; study for the test that is coming up tomorrow; study for the test that's
the furthest away; go through each class in order and focus on materials that haven't changed; or in
the case of OPT, don't study at all because my OPT algorithm isn't optimal at all...

    After comparing the 5 algorithms, I have reached the conclusion that surprisingly random isn't the
worst. FIFO has the worst performance of all the algorithms, with random coming in at a close second. LRU
and Clock are similar in performance, and when OPT works, its the optimal method by definition.

    For LRU, the hit rate increases as the memory increases, due to there being more slots available to
hold more older data, thus increasing the chance for a hit. Compared to other algorithms, increasing
memory usually increases hit rate as well. For a large trace like Matmul memory size affects hit rate
greatly, allowing a 25% increase in hit rate for only a 50% increase in memory.
